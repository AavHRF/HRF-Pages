FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions for the Attorney-General

Can I press charges on my own against another citizen?

No. You must request that the state press charges.

After requesting that the state press charges, the Crown Attorney-General will review the evidence, and decide whether or not to proceed with any given case.

I'm a member of HM Government, and looking for legal advice. Can the Crown Attorney-General supply it?

The Crown Attorney-General is tasked with advising the Crown as to the legality of its actions. This advice extends to HM Government, as long as the advice is related to their office, and not of a personal nature.
